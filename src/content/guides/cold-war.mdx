## What this guide will cover

The Cold War is the longest, strangest war in modern history. No declared fight between the U.S. and USSR, but proxy wars on every continent, an arms race that nearly ended civilization, and a cultural standoff that shaped music, sports, science, and the internet you're reading this on.

This guide is being built right now. When it ships it will walk you through:

- **Origins, 1945 to 1949.** Yalta and Potsdam, the Iron Curtain speech, the Truman Doctrine, the Marshall Plan, the Berlin Blockade and Airlift, the founding of NATO and the partition of Germany.
- **The first hot proxy: Korea, 1950 to 1953.** Kim Il-sung's invasion, MacArthur's gamble at Inchon, the Chinese intervention, and the armistice that still defines the peninsula today.
- **Atomic age and the Red Scare.** The hydrogen bomb, McCarthyism, the Rosenbergs, and the doctrine of <KeyTerm term="MAD">Mutually Assured Destruction, the idea that nuclear war is unwinnable because both sides die.</KeyTerm>
- **The space race.** Sputnik (1957), Yuri Gagarin (1961), the moon landing (1969), and how the U.S. turned a humiliation into a national project.
- **Cuba, 1961 to 1962.** The Bay of Pigs disaster, Khrushchev's missile gambit, the thirteen days that came closer to nuclear war than any other in history.
- **Vietnam, 1955 to 1975.** Why containment policy pulled the U.S. in, how the Tet Offensive turned American opinion, and why Vietnam ended in the only way it could.
- **Détente, 1969 to 1979.** SALT, Nixon in China, the Helsinki Accords, the brief thaw before Afghanistan froze it again.
- **The endgame, 1980 to 1991.** Reagan's military buildup, glasnost and perestroika, Solidarity in Poland, the fall of the Wall (1989), and the Soviet collapse on Christmas Day, 1991.

<Callout type="insight" title="Why the Cold War still matters">
  Almost every geopolitical conflict on the news today, NATO expansion, the South China Sea, Iran, Korea, Russia and Ukraine, Cuba, has roots that lead back to a Cold War decision made between 1945 and 1991. Understanding the original logic is the cheapest way to make sense of the present.
</Callout>

## What it will look like when shipped

Same density and component depth as the WWII guide:

- A 3D Cesium globe with capitals, alliances (NATO blue, Warsaw Pact red), proxy war markers, and the Berlin Wall trace
- Interactive timeline scrubber from 1945 to 1991
- Side-by-side ideology cheat sheet (capitalism vs communism vs the lived reality of each)
- A worked Cuban Missile Crisis decision-tree (you play Kennedy, Khrushchev, or Castro)
- Speech-graded recall on key terms in English, plus translated key Russian phrases (glasnost, perestroika, Politburo)
- Five practice quiz banks, "↻ New quiz" button to cycle them, with answers spread evenly across A through D
- Mock EOC-style timed practice section
- The full flashcard SRS deck
