## What just got added

Every guide now has three optional AI tools that you can drop in. They are powered by **WebLLM**, an open-source library that runs a real language model (Llama, Qwen, Phi) entirely inside your browser. The first time you load a model, your laptop downloads it once (about 700 MB to 2 GB depending on which model) and caches it. After that, everything runs offline.

<Callout type="insight" title="Why this is special">
Most "AI tutors" send your questions to a server, charge per token, and require an internet connection. This one runs the model on your own GPU. No keys, no costs, no data leaves your laptop. It works on a plane.
</Callout>

## 1. The chat tutor

Drop `<AITutor />` into any guide. It auto-reads the surrounding study guide and answers questions grounded in that material.

<AITutor subject="Getting Started" />

Try clicking **Quiz me**, **5 key takeaways**, or just type your own question. It will stream the answer one token at a time. Hit the red square to stop generating mid-stream.

## 2. Fresh practice question generator

`<AIQuizGen count={5} />` reads the same guide content and writes brand-new multiple choice questions every time you click. The questions look identical to the static `<Quiz>` blocks but they are different every run, so you can drill the same material from new angles forever.

<AIQuizGen count={3} subject="Getting Started" />

## 3. The "explain simpler" wrapper

Wrap any dense paragraph in `<AIExplain>` and a button appears that re-states it at three reading levels: ELI5, ELI15 (default), or "go deeper".

<AIExplain level="eli15" topic="WebGPU and in-browser inference">
WebLLM uses WebGPU, a low-level graphics and compute API, to perform matrix multiplications and attention operations on the GPU. Quantized model weights (typically 4-bit) are streamed from disk through the GPU's unified memory, enabling consumer hardware to run multi-billion-parameter language models at interactive token rates without contacting a remote server.
</AIExplain>

Click "Explain simpler" above and watch it rewrite the paragraph for a 9th-grader.

## How to add these to your own guide

```mdx
---
title: "Your guide"
subject: "Math"

---

import AITutor from '../../components/AITutor.astro';
import AIQuizGen from '../../components/AIQuizGen.astro';
import AIExplain from '../../components/AIExplain.astro';

## Your content here
...

## Practice
<AIQuizGen count={5} />

## Need help?
<AITutor />
```

That's it. The components auto-discover the guide's prose on the page, so you don't have to pass content manually.

## Choosing a model

The picker at the top of each `<AITutor>` lets students switch between five models. Defaults to **Llama 3.2 1B** (700 MB, fastest). For tougher subjects (organic chem, multivariable calc), upgrade to **Qwen 2.5 1.5B** (best at math/code) or **Phi 3.5 mini** (best academic reasoning).

| Model | Size | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| Llama 3.2 1B | ~700 MB | Quick Q&A, fast on Chromebooks |
| Qwen 2.5 1.5B | ~1.0 GB | Math, science, code |
| Llama 3.2 3B | ~2.0 GB | Long-form study help |
| Phi 3.5 mini | ~2.4 GB | Academic content, deep reasoning |
| Hermes 3 | ~2.0 GB | Conversational tutor persona |

The selection is sticky per device, saved in localStorage.

## Requirements

- **WebGPU** (Chrome, Edge, Arc, Brave on desktop or laptop). Safari and most mobile browsers do not yet support it.
- A GPU with at least 2 GB free VRAM for the smaller models.
- One-time download of the model (cached after).

## Privacy

Nothing about your conversation, the guide content, your IP, or any other data ever leaves the browser. The Network tab will show only the model weight download, and only on the first visit.

## When NOT to use AI

The AI is a tutor, not a textbook. Use it to:

- Re-explain something you didn't follow
- Generate fresh practice
- Ask "why" follow-up questions

Don't use it to:

- Replace doing the deep dive yourself
- Cite as a source on essays
- Learn material the guide doesn't already cover (it can hallucinate)

The deep-dive text is still the source of truth. The AI is here to drill it deeper into your memory.
